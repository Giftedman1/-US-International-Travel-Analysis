# US-International-Travel-Analysis

An exploratory data analysis project examining 32 years of US international outbound travel data to uncover trends in destinations, travel timing, seasonality, and pandemic-era shifts in passenger behavior.

---

## Project Overview

This project analyzes 4.1 billion passenger journeys from the United States to 46 international destinations over a 32 year period. Using Excel, the analysis explores how travel patterns vary across destinations, weekdays, months, quarters, and major global disruptions such as COVID-19.

The goal of this project was to identify long term travel behavior trends and generate actionable insights relevant to the travel, tourism, and aviation industries.

---

## Objectives

This analysis set out to answer five specific questions:

- What are the most traveled US destinations overall?
- How do passenger volumes vary by weekday?
- How do passenger volumes vary by month?
- How did US travel destinations change between 2020 and 2021?
- What do passenger volumes look like on a quarterly basis?

---

## Dataset

The dataset covers 32 years of US international outbound travel records.

### Column Description

| Column | Description |
|---|---|
| `destination` | Name of the country passengers traveled to from the United States |
| `passengers` | Total number of passengers recorded for that destination |
| `year` | Year the travel occurred |
| `month` | Month the travel took place |
| `weekday` | Day of the week the travel occurred |
| `quarter` | Three month period grouped as Q1, Q2, Q3, and Q4 |

## Key Findings

### Canada, Mexico, and the UK Dominate Overall

Across the full 32 year period, Canada leads all destinations with 601 million passengers, followed by Mexico at 524 million and the United Kingdom at 473 million. Japan, Germany, and France round out the top six.

This suggests that proximity and strong cultural ties heavily influence where Americans choose to travel.

---

### The Pandemic Reshuffled the Top Destinations

Comparing 2020 and 2021 revealed a significant shift in travel behavior.

- In 2020, Mexico, Canada, and the UK received over 43% of all US outbound passengers, totaling 25 million travelers.
- In 2021, Mexico, Dominican Republic, and Colombia together received over 56% of passengers, totaling 17 million travelers.

The sharp decline reflects the continued impact of COVID-19 restrictions, while the rise of destinations like the Dominican Republic and Colombia suggests travelers prioritized destinations with fewer entry restrictions.

---

### Americans Prefer Weekend Travel

Passenger volumes by weekday show that:

- Sunday is the busiest travel day with 614 million passengers
- Saturday follows closely with 601 million passengers
- Monday and Wednesday record the lowest passenger volumes

This indicates that most travelers prefer weekend departures to maximize leisure time without affecting weekday work schedules.

---

### Summer is Peak Travel Season

Monthly travel patterns show strong seasonality:

- Travel begins increasing in May
- Peaks in July at 410 million passengers
- August follows closely at 408 million passengers
- Passenger volume declines sharply after summer and reaches its lowest point in November at 309 million passengers

On a quarterly basis:

- Q3 is the strongest travel quarter
- Q3 and Q4 each exceed 1 billion passengers
- Average monthly passenger volume across the dataset is 349 million

These findings highlight the importance of summer demand planning for airlines, hotels, and travel agencies.

---

## Opportunities and Implications

This analysis provides several actionable insights for the travel industry:

- Airlines can improve capacity planning around weekend departure spikes
- Tourism boards can align marketing campaigns ahead of peak Q3 demand
- Travel agencies can develop seasonal travel packages during high demand periods
- Destinations can position themselves as accessible alternatives during periods of global uncertainty or travel restrictions

The 2021 travel patterns also demonstrate how quickly travelers adapt when restrictions disrupt traditional travel behavior.

---

## Conclusion

Thirty two years of travel data reveals a consistent pattern in US outbound travel behavior. Americans travel most during summer, prefer weekend departures, and tend to favor nearby or culturally familiar destinations.

Canada, Mexico, and the United Kingdom have remained dominant destinations over time, although disruptions such as the COVID-19 pandemic can rapidly reshape global travel patterns.

For stakeholders in aviation, tourism, and hospitality, understanding these trends is essential for smarter planning, stronger campaigns, and more targeted customer strategies.
